Digital Kitchen Scale Dissection Project

Taylor Digital Kitchen Scale (Model 5273821)

1. Introduction

Micah George, Computer Engineering Major.

I have always had a deep interest in robotics-related electives and projects throughout both high school and college. I chose to dissect the Taylor Digital Kitchen Scale (Model 5273821) because it demonstrates several key embedded system concepts: sensor interfacing, signal processing and digital filtering, microcontroller usage with LCD interfaces, and principles of low-power design.

2. Product Market and Retail Information

  • Product History: Taylor has been producing precision measurement instruments for over 150 years. This digital kitchen scale is part of their home appliance product line.
  • MSRP and Street Prices: MSRP typically around $25, can be found online for as low as $15–$20.
  • Retail Outlets: Available at Amazon, Walmart, Target, and Taylor's official site.
  • Similar Models and Rebranded/OEM Versions: The Taylor Digital Kitchen Scale (Model 5273821) has been observed in similar form under various private label brands, particularly those found in big-box retailers and kitchenware stores. Taylor is known to supply products that are cosmetically or slightly functionally altered for rebranding purposes. A closely related model, the Taylor 5270842, shares nearly identical internal hardware and user interface, suggesting a shared design platform optimized for varied market segments or exclusive retail partnerships.

4. Product Operation

  • Human-machine interfaces: Power/Tare button, Unit button, Hold button, and an LCD screen for measurement readouts.
  • Physical components: Includes a removable stainless steel scale tray for easy cleaning and precise weight distribution.
  • Power requirements: Operates using 3 AAA batteries, housed in a rear compartment secured with a small screw for safety and tamper resistance.
